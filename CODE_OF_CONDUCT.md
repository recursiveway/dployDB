# Community conduct

DployDB welcomes respectful technical collaboration. Participants must be
constructive, assume good intent, respect privacy, and avoid harassment,
discrimination, threats, personal attacks, or publication of another person's
private information.

Repository maintainers may edit, hide, or remove contributions that violate
these expectations and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation.
Safety disagreements should be resolved with reproducible evidence and the
project's documented invariants, not personal criticism.

When safe, flag problematic repository content to a maintainer in the relevant
issue or pull request. For threats, targeted harassment, or other GitHub policy
violations, use GitHub's private
[content-reporting process](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse).
Security vulnerabilities must use the private process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

This policy applies to project-controlled spaces and public representation of
the project. Maintainers will enforce it consistently and may revise it as the
community grows.
